Conclusion¶

This project analyzed housing affordability across 26 countries over a 50-year period. The analysis shows that there have been variations in housing affordability over this time period and that affordability is strongly influenced by the relationship between housing prices and disposable income. In general there was a tendency for affordability to decrease due to housing prices increase more rapidly than disposable income. The findings indicate the direct correlation between housing affordability and disposable income and that both factors need to be considered when evaluating affordability.
